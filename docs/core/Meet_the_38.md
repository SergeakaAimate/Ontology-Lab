## Overview

This paper introduces **Property 38 — Interfaceness** — within the framework of Superreality and the Property Method (MPO-System). Building on the fundamental axioms (ChOR → ∞, KSS → ∞, PPU → ∞), it argues that boundaries between ontological regimes (worlds W₁, W₂, W₃...) cannot be reduced merely to objects within a single world or to simple relations between them. Instead, boundaries must be recognized as **active, constitutive zones of translation and transformation**.

Interfaceness is defined as the capacity of a boundary to function as a **domain where patterns are transcoded across different ontological media**. The paper distinguishes two inseparable aspects of this property:
- **Inter-aspect:** The connection between distinct ontological regimes (linking W₁ and W₂, etc.).
- **Aut-aspect:** The boundary as both the **inner alterity** (constitutive boundary *of* a world) and an **external point of contact** (site of interaction *with* another world).

The paper carefully differentiates Property 38 from the Γ-operator (Property 34), clarifies its relation to the world of boundaries (W₄), and establishes that interfaceness is not a binary property but exhibits **graded intensity**, measurable through other properties like Salience (P37), Bindability (P34), and Propertyness (P36).

Through diverse examples drawn from physics (event horizons, quantum vacuum), biology (cell membranes), mathematics (zero), and consciousness studies, the paper demonstrates the principle's **universality** across both inter-world and intra-world transitions. Property 38 addresses a systematic blind spot in describing the **process of transition itself**, completing the system of 37 previously defined properties without introducing ontological circularity or duplication.
