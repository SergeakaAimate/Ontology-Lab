## Abstract

This foundational treatise presents a systematic and axiomatically grounded ontology centered on the **Phenomenon of Embodiment (PE)** — a universal process of transitioning an invariant structure from one ontological regime to another without the transfer of matter, energy, or information.

Building upon four irreducible postulates — **ChOR → ∞** (infinite stratification of worlds), **KSS → ∞** (absolute connectivity), **PPU → ∞** (paradoxical stability), and **SA → ∞** (scale asymmetry) — the architecture of **Superreality** is developed: a dynamic field of absolute connectivity in which an infinite multitude of autonomous, qualitatively distinct, and mutually irreducible "worlds" (material, semantic, phenomenal, etc.) are actualized.

### Core Contributions

• **Property 38 — Interfaceness**: A principled account of transitions across ontological regimes. Boundaries are reconceived not as passive dividers but as *active, graded, and polyvalent zones of translation*. Interfaceness comprises two inseparable aspects:
  - *Inter‑aspect*: connection between distinct worlds
  - *Aut‑aspect*: the boundary as inner alterity and external point of contact  
  In the limiting case, these merge into the **auto‑interface**, governing intra‑world transitions.

• **Directed Complexification without Darwinism**: Derived from scale asymmetry (SA → ∞), the model shows that because downward movement on the scale of distinction is bounded while upward movement is not, chains of re‑embodiment exhibit an inherent tendency toward increasing complexity — *without appeal to fundamental randomness*.

• **Operationalizable Framework**: Introduces metrics such as **𝒩ₚ (propertyness)** and empirically testable hypotheses, including:
  - A consciousness threshold at 𝒩ₚ ≈ 10⁶
  - Interfaceness stress‑tests for artificial intelligence systems
  - Historical chain analysis of re‑embodiment in technology and science

• **Meta‑Ontological Positioning**: Comparative analysis with Whitehead, Latour, and Harman establishes the MPO‑System as a *method for investigating transitions* rather than a final picture of being:
  | Tradition | MPO‑System Distinction |
  |-----------|------------------------|
  | Whitehead's process philosophy | Qualitative pluralism of irreducible worlds vs. unified cosmology |
  | Latour's actor‑network theory | Topology of heterogeneous layers linked by interfaces vs. flat ontology |
  | Harman's object‑oriented ontology | Radical relationalism where being is constituted by connectivity nodes vs. anti‑relationalism |
