## 📋 Abstract

This essay establishes **Scale Asymmetry (SA → ∞)** as the fourth axiom of the **MPO-System** (Method of Properties — Ontological Operating System). It argues that the directed asymmetry of scale—a finite limit when decreasing scale ("inward") and no limit when increasing scale ("outward")—is not an empirical property but a condition for the possibility of distinction itself.

Through operational phenomenology, the analysis demonstrates SA → ∞ across material physics (Planck scale vs. cosmological horizon), mathematics (irrational number hierarchies), and phenomenal experience (the structure of the "I"). The axiom redefines foundational "hard problems" (quantum gravity, consciousness, AI) as boundary transitions between regimes of distinction rather than searches for unified laws.
