### Abstract
This essay examines the heuristic potential of scientific analogies through the "paradox of radii" arising from the rubber-sheet model of General Relativity. The paradox—concerning the relationship between a massive body and its gravitational well—serves as a lens for analyzing how analogies function as cognitive tools that reveal structural connections between ontological layers. We propose that analogies generate entangled pairs between domains (mechanical, geometric, semantic), enabling circulation of meanings governed by a *Law of Conservation of Meanings*. This framework is applied to the black hole information paradox, the hard problem of consciousness, and AI, suggesting that ontological entanglement offers pathways for theoretical unification within a multi-layered "Superreality."
