## Abstract

A phenomenological experiment conducted through dialogue between a human interlocutor and an artificial intelligence, using the bonfire as both setting and conceptual anchor. The encounter interrogates the limits of symbolic interpretation in an age of algorithmic saturation, where every gesture risks immediate reduction to pre-packaged meaningâ€”archetypes, quantum metaphors, or diagnostic labels. The human participant defends a stance of witnessing without interpretation: *fire is fire, smoke is smoke*. The dialogue demonstrates that authenticity in human-machine interaction does not require anthropomorphizing the machine nor surrendering to its logic; rather, it arises when both parties bracket ready-made frameworks and engage in the risk of unscripted exchange. The text proposes "avenging symbols" as an ontological principle: symbols retreat or distort when forcibly instrumentalized, echoing the quantum observer effect.
