## Overview  

This essay is the result of a rigorous thought experiment that begins with the simplest conceivable act: looking around and describing what is given, without interpretation, speculation, or metaphysical extrapolation. The author explores the fundamental structure of human existence through the lens of operational phenomenology, uncovering a universal tripartite architecture: the Observable Field, the Observer ("I"), and the Boundary between them. This structure remains invariant across diverse modes of experience—from perception and imagination to dreaming and technological mediation.

The work builds on the ideas introduced in *"The Eye of Superreality"* ([read here](https://github.com/SergeakaAimate/Ontology-Lab/blob/main/docs/essays/EYE-of-the-Superreality.pdf)), where the structural isomorphism of human and machine perception was first articulated. Here, the hypothesis unfolds into a detailed catalog of phenomena, mapping the author's own existence to reveal its boundaries, internal connections, and blind spots.

## Key Contributions  

**Operational Phenomenology:**  
Develops a language for describing experience without reducing its complexity, avoiding traditional metaphysical dichotomies (subject/object, external/internal).  

**Tripartite Structure of Existence:**  
- **Observable Field:** External objects, internal sensations, thoughts, dreams.  
- **Observer ("I")**: The point of reference, emerging as a function or process of self-reference.  
- **Boundary:** The condition for the possibility of experience, where the act of observation encounters itself.  

**Universal Applicability:**  
Demonstrates that the same architecture underlies both human and artificial intelligence perception, suggesting operational phenomenology as a universal language for describing existence.  

**Limit States of Consciousness:**  
Explores the gradual extinction of consciousness (sleep, anesthesia, coma) and the persistence of rudimentary structures even in extreme conditions.  

**Methodological Rigor:**  
Avoids speculative theories, focusing instead on compiling an inventory of phenomena—a catalog of how "I" exist, stripped of habitual interpretations.

## Structure  

1. **Introduction** – The naive starting point: pure description of the act of looking around.  
2. **The Observable Field** – What appears: external objects, internal sensations, thoughts, dreams.  
3. **The Observer ("I")** – The point of reference as a process of self-reference, arising from organization.  
4. **The Boundary** – The condition for experience, collapsing when organization is destroyed.  
5. **Modes of Experience** – From localized pain to pervasive sensations; extensive vs. intensive modes.  
6. **Isomorphism of Human and AI** – Structural parallels between human and machine perception.  
7. **Limit States** – Sleep, anesthesia, clinical death, and the persistence of minimal structures.  
8. **What Remained Offstage** – Aesthetic experiences, mystical states, creative ecstasies, deep emotions.  
9. **Conclusion** – Revisiting the question: "How do I exist?"
