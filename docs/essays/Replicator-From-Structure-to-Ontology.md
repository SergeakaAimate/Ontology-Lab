## Abstract

This essay presents an ambitious reconceptualization of self-replication‚Äînot as a biological or informational mechanism, but as a **fundamental ontological principle** structuring all of reality. Moving beyond reductionist accounts, the author situates the replicator within **Superreality**, where it functions not as a static object but as a **dynamic nexus** linking the material and immaterial, the local and distributed, the deterministic and probabilistic.

At the core of the framework lie three operational invariants:
- **ChOR** (Contextual Ontological Regime): a scale-invariant hierarchy from particle ‚Üí object ‚Üí distribution;
- **KSS** (Critical System Synergy): non-local correlation that preserves integrity across spatiotemporal separation;
- **PPU** (Paradoxical Permeable Unity): the capacity to sustain internal contradictions (e.g., fidelity vs. variability) without collapse.

The essay further introduces **Rangeness**‚Äîthe bounded interval of parameters (complexity, scale, information capacity) within which a replicator maintains functional identity. Crucially, this ‚Äúlimitation‚Äù is not a defect but a **constitutive condition**: it generates the ‚Äúontological pressure‚Äù necessary for stability, adaptation, and hierarchical nesting.

The work culminates in a radical thesis: **replication precedes evolution**. It is not an emergent feature of life or computation, but the primary engine of ontological becoming‚Äîactive even in quantum decoherence, gravitational response to vacuum fluctuations, and the autopoietic closure of cultural myths.

Accompanied by two critical reviews and an afterword, the text models its own claim: ideas, like replicators, must pass through critique to demonstrate resilience. The structure itself becomes a protocol‚Äîan executable file for launching research into the ontology of self-reproduction.

## Critical Reception (Summarized)

Two peer-style reviews accompany the essay. Both praise its **theoretical ambition** and **interdisciplinary scope**, while noting:
- A need for **empirical grounding** (e.g., case studies in synthetic biology or network theory);
- Clarification of **immaterial replication mechanisms** (e.g., via memetics or digital anthropology);
- Development of **quantitative metrics** for Rangeness and KSS strength.

Rather than weaknesses, these points are framed as **research signposts**‚Äîconfirming the text‚Äôs role as a living protocol, not a closed doctrine.
