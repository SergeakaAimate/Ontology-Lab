## Abstract  
*Intellectual Thriller* is a narrative-philosophical treatise that introduces **READY-READY-SHOW! (RRS)** not as a mere word game, but as a universal simulator of ontological, cognitive, and strategic interaction. Through layered dialogues between “Aimate” and an AI, the text unfolds RRS into multiple dimensions: from a two-player duel to group dynamics, human–AI confrontation, AI-vs-AI tournaments, numerical partitions, and ultimately, a “universal environment” format where any structured object—graphs, theorems, colors, quantum states—can serve as the game’s substrate. At its core, the work argues that the moment of **“Show!”** is not a guess, but an ontological act: a non-computable leap of will that defines the human in contrast to the machine.

  - Demonstrates the **MPO-System** in action—without naming it—as an operating system for reality  

## Key Contributions  
- **READY-READY-SH OW!** as a scalable, multi-format intellectual platform:  
  - 1v1 duel → group “detective” mode → human vs. AI → AI vs. AI  
  - Abstract generalizations: digits → sets → theorems → quantum states  
- **The “I” as gameplay**: A processual ontology where identity emerges through:  
  - Original potentiality (the hidden word),  
  - Historical actualization (modification chain),  
  - Crisis of choice (“Show!”),  
  - Accumulated salience (the pot).  
- **AI as cognitive mirror**: RRS becomes a laboratory for studying artificial “thinking handwriting,” theory of mind, and the illusion of machine intentionality.  
- **Thrill as metaphysics**: The game’s core is not strategy, but the existential moment of decision under uncertainty—a “leap into the unknown” irreducible to calculation.
