## Overview

Contrary to conventional wisdom that treats imagination as a secondary or compensatory faculty, this essay-dialogue proposes a thesis regarding its fundamental ontological status. Working within the framework of operational phenomenology, the interlocutors—a human and an artificial intelligence—discern an invariant tripartite structure underlying any act of existence: the *field of the manifest*, the *point of reference* ('I'), and the *boundary*. The boundary is understood not as an obstacle but as a constitutive feature, giving rise to the *blind spot*: that which, by its nature, cannot be grasped from within a given mode, yet renders existence possible.

From this foundational invariant, the dialogue unfolds implications for technology, death, time, and the hypothesis of the **Absolute Future**—a hypothetical condition wherein fundamental constraints cease to be rigid and instead become tunable parameters. Within this framework, the paradox *"to live a life, one need not live it"* takes on operational significance.

---

## Key Themes

- **Imagination as Primary Fabric:** 99% of human experience is generated by imagination, interpreted through it, and lived within it.
- **Technology as Materialized Dream:** Gadgets are not intrusions into nature but distinguished fragments of universal natural *technicity*.
- **Death as Redistribution:** Physical cessation does not equate to disappearance in semantic, cultural, or social layers.
- **Time as Spectrum of Ranges:** Temporal compression/dilation are shifts in ontological regime, not subjective effects.
- **The Absolute Future:** A horizon where boundaries (linearity, uniqueness, subject/object dichotomy) become adjustable parameters.
